Educational Philosphy

It is with great fortune that I have been able to work throughout my career in multiple educational organizations providing me with a myriad of experiences. My current and previous schools truly bring educational rigor to a whole new level. As a teacher, this helped me develop my craft and push my limits, along with the limits of my students, to new heights. As a leader, I have helped teachers and students use a culture of excellence to spawn innovation in the classroom and through co-curricular activities. The benefits of the academic challenges found in these schools are many: students perform well on external assessments, are recognized around the world for their academic achievements, and matriculate to top universities. After all, a rigorous school culture demands hard work and a level of excellence in everything, and as a result, excellence is often exactly what results.

However, a culture of rigor can come at a cost as it can limit organizations from truly meeting students' holistic needs. A rigorous school culture has the potential to burn out faculty members, force students to compromise their academic integrity and create serious conflict within families. International schools should strive to provide adequate programs to support students with ESL and Learning Support needs and include a wider variety of students and families, so more students can receive a top tier education. Great schools have programs in place allowing educators to support all students without sacrificing rigor.

Education is about holistic growth, and my dream is to work at a school that recognizes individual needs within a culture of excellence. As a leader, I create an environment that fosters honest conversations and expertise centered on student needs. I work every day to shift conversations from an elitist private school mentality to what really matters, meeting the needs of all of our students through pastoral care. I believe I have contributed to significant progress around meeting student needs by making students the focus of all we do. My philosophy is centered on the balance of holistic support and rigor.
